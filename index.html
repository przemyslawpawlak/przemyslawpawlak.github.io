// 01 — About

Sharp eyes.
Sharper questions.

I've been breaking software professionally for over 20 years — and I still find it satisfying every time something fails in a way nobody expected. Good QA isn't about following a checklist. It's about understanding what the application is supposed to do, what it actually does, and why the gap between those two things matters.

My background spans manual testing, test planning, defect analysis, and documentation review. I'm currently building out my automation skillset with Selenium, Postman, and Python — because the best testers in the next decade will be the ones who can do both.

I'm based in Victoria, BC and open to remote and hybrid opportunities across Canada. My longer term interest is in AI quality assurance and auditing — a field that needs people who can think critically about system behavior, not just run scripts against it.
